Key Principles of Diversity and Inclusion

Equity & Fairness

Equity ensures that all individuals have access to the same opportunities, while fairness means treating people justly according to their unique circumstances. Unlike equality, which gives everyone the same resources, equity acknowledges that people have different needs and barriers.

Belonging & Respect

Creating an environment where everyone feels respected, valued, and like they belong is foundational to inclusion. When people feel they belong, they are more likely to contribute, collaborate, and stay engaged.

Representation & Participation

Diversity is not just about demographics—it's about whose voices are heard and whose experiences are reflected. Representation enhances creativity, innovation, and problem-solving by integrating a broader range of viewpoints.

Best Practices for Diversity and Inclusion

1. Embed D&I in Leadership and Accountability

Without leadership commitment, D&I efforts often stall. Leaders set the tone, shape culture, and control resources.

  • Make D&I a leadership priority with measurable goals.
  • Include inclusive leadership in manager training and reviews.
  • Publicly champion diverse voices and recognize inclusive behavior.

2. Use Data to Drive Equity and Change

Data brings objectivity, reveals disparities, and helps track real progress—not just intentions.

  • Analyze hiring, promotion, and pay by demographic groups.
  • Run regular inclusion surveys and act on the insights.
  • Share D&I data transparently to build trust.

3. Foster an Inclusive, Belonging-Focused Culture

People won’t stay—or thrive—without belonging. Culture is what people experience daily.

  • Build psychologically safe spaces where all voices are heard.
  • Support ERGs, inclusive events, and accessible workspaces.
  • Celebrate diversity authentically and consistently, not just during heritage months.
